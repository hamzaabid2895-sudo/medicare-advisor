Understand Your Medicare Options

Medicare has four parts. Each one covers something different. Here's what you need to know — in plain English:

H
A

Hospital Insurance

Covers inpatient hospital stays, skilled nursing facility care, hospice, and some home health care. Most people pay $0 per month.

M
B

Medical Insurance

Covers doctor visits, lab tests, and preventive services. Part B requires a monthly premium. In 2025, the standard amount is $185/month.

C
C

Medicare Advantage

A private plan that bundles Parts A, B, and D into one plan. Often includes extra benefits like dental, vision, and hearing.

D
D

Drug Coverage

Covers prescription medications. Offered by private insurance companies. Costs and covered drugs vary by plan.

Parts A and B together are called Original Medicare. From there, you have two main paths: add a Medigap plan plus a Part D plan — or switch to a Medicare Advantage plan that bundles it all.

Medicare Advantage Plans

All-in-One Coverage with benefits Original Medicare doesn't offer. Medicare Advantage (Part C) is the most popular choice for seniors who want comprehensive care.

What Is Part C?

Private insurance companies handle your coverage directly, replacing your Original Medicare card with an all-in-one approach. It typically includes Parts A, B, and D, plus extras.

🦷

Dental Coverage

Routine cleanings, X-rays, fillings, and dentures. Original Medicare won't touch this, but Advantage often includes it.

👁️

Vision Coverage

Annual eye exams, eyeglass allowances, and contact lens benefits. Critical care for healthy aging.

👂

Hearing Coverage

Hearing tests and aid allowances. Can save you $3,000–$7,000 out of pocket compared to Original Medicare.

💪

SilverSneakers

Fitness memberships and local gym access to promote an active lifestyle at no extra cost.

HMO vs. PPO: Choose Your Network

HMO

Health Maintenance Organization

  • Lower monthly premiums (often $0)
  • Lower copays for in-network care
  • Must use in-network doctors
  • Referrals needed for specialists
PPO

Preferred Provider Organization

  • Freedom to see any Medicare doctor
  • No referrals needed
  • Out-of-network care covered (at higher cost)
  • Ideal for travelers and snowbirds

Medicare Supplement (Medigap)

Stop worrying about the medical bills Medicare doesn't cover. A Medigap plan fills the 20% "gap" left by Original Medicare.

📌 The 20% Gap: If you have a $50,000 surgery, Original Medicare pays $40,000. You owe $10,000. A Medigap plan can cover that entire bill for you.
